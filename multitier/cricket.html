England steady innings

England rebounded from a stuttering start to their innings to reach 4 for 156 in the 35th over of their run chase in the opening tri-series final against Australia at the MCG this evening.

After being set 253 for victory, the tourists stumbled to be 3 for 15 in the sixth over before a 133-run partnership between Paul Collingwood and Ian Bell resurrected the English tilt.

Collingwood was unbeaten on 73 in the 35th over while Andrew Flintoff was on 2. To keep up-to-date, visit our live scorecard.

Healy under fire for breast cancer gesture

Former Test star turned television commentator Ian Healy was forced to apologise for making an insensitive gesture as Australian all-rounder Andrew Symonds spoke about breast cancer today.

Healey, who earned 119 Test caps as Australian wicketkeeper, pretended he was playing a violin as Symonds explained in the commentary box that he was playing with a pink bat grip in order to raise awareness about breast cancer.
