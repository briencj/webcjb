3. Fundamental difference between split-plot and two-phase experiments
Split-plots involve independent randomizations
reducible to a single randomization
Two-phase experiments involve composed randomizations, at least
not reducible to a single randomization
Independent randomizations for the standard split-plot
Looks like two randomizations but reducible
Can do by applying one of allowable permutations of Blocks, Main plots and Sub-plots to combinations of A and B
Single randomization for the standard split-plot
One randomization  two tiers (not three)
Composed randomizations
Initial randomization of tier 3 onto tier 2.
Tier 2 is then randomized onto tier 1.
Composed randomizations in the two-phase biodiversity experiment
Involves two randomizations:
treatments to field units and
field units to laboratory units.
4. The secret to designing two-phase experiments
They involve composed and, sometimes, coincident randomizations
Divide the factors into tiers
For each randomization, when working out design to employ
consider only factors from the 2 tiers directly involved
Consider balanced designs
whole experiment will be balanced  (Brien & Bailey, unpublished)

5. Summary
Multitiered experiments involve irreducible multiple randomizations.
Two-phase experiments are an important class, particularly those involving field and laboratory phases
Consider randomizing at lab phase with appropriate blocking and possibly utilizing nonorthogonal, balanced designs or other
Specifying tiers:  forces consideration of all factors and randomization;
Can use randomization to derive ANOVA models and tables that exhibit confounding
